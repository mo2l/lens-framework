# LENS Glossary

Alphabetical glossary of all LENS-specific terminology.

---

**AI Influence Log** — Record of how AI framing shaped the conversation, included in synthesis. Captures what the AI did, whether the human caught it, and how it affected final positions.

**Arc** — The sequence of rounds in a session. Default: Definition → Internal → External → Adoption → Risks.

**Carry Forward** — Routing unresolved questions or topics to future rounds as first-class inputs.

**Checkpoint** — Explicit pivot-or-stop decision point after Round 1. The AI asks whether the question is still right before proceeding.

**Challenger** — The AI's role: applies systematic pressure through lenses. Never commits — always pressures.

**Cluster** — Grouped related questions by theme within a round, so answers can build on each other.

**Commitment** — What was decided; the core of a position. Every round produces commitments, not summaries.

**Context** — Background information in SCOPE.md that enables effective challenges.

**Conviction Shift** — A position the human entered with but no longer holds after the session.

**Emergent Question** — New question spawned by answers during a round; carried forward as first-class input to future rounds.

**Experience Level** — Expert | Moderate | Exploring. Determines challenge intensity and AI adaptation.

**False Tension** — Apparent contradiction that dissolves under examination. Named explicitly so it doesn't keep resurfacing.

**Follow-up Review** — Post-session check (2–4 weeks later) on unresolved items and conviction durability.

**Forcing Question** — How a lens is expressed; creates productive discomfort when applied honestly.

**Inversion Challenge** — In the final round, constructing the strongest case for the opposite of consensus before synthesis.

**LENS** — Lensed Exploration, Narrowing & Synthesis. The framework name.

**Lens** — A persistent pressure-test perspective that catches a specific failure mode. Each scope defines 4–8 lenses.

**Lens Review** — Human-initiated audit during the write-up phase where the human reviews which lenses the AI applied each round and flags any steering.

**Lens Coverage Map** — Defines which lenses are primary/secondary for each round type within a scope.

**Per-Round Lens Limit** — Maximum of 4 lenses applied per round. Prevents fatigue and diluted focus.

**Position** — A concrete commitment with reasoning — not a summary of discussion. Includes the commitment, reasoning, and connections.

**Primary Lens** — Always applied in a given round type (within the 4-lens-per-round limit).

**Reasoning** — Why this commitment and not the alternatives; part of every position.

**Reopen Check** — After capturing positions, checking if new insights contradict prior rounds. Flagged positions are briefly re-examined.

**Reopen Limit** — A position can be reopened once. After re-examination, it is either revised or locked. Further challenges must be named as unresolved tensions.

**Round** — A structured discussion cycle following the five-step protocol: cluster → challenge → respond → capture → close.

**Scope** — A decision or challenge to explore; isolated in its own directory with its own SCOPE.md.

**SCOPE.md** — Configuration file defining topic, lenses, arc, context, and experience level for a session.

**Secondary Lens** — Applied only when clearly relevant, within the 4-lens-per-round limit.

**Round Duration (Time-boxing)** — Guideline of 20–40 minutes per round. If exceeded without converging, the AI offers to park unresolved topics.

**Synthesis** — Final document produced after all rounds complete. Includes positions, AI influence log, and conviction shifts.

**Tension Resolution** — How conflicting positions are reconciled: resolved, unresolved, or false tension.

**Write-up Phase** — Documentation happens after all rounds, never during. Prevents premature convergence.
